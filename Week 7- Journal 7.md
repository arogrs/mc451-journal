# Weekly Journal Entry Guide

## How to Approach Your Reflection

Each week, you'll reflect on course material using **one of three approaches**. Pick the one that connects most to what you're thinking about this week.

---

## The Three Approaches

### 🔗 The Connector
**Definition:** Link this week's material to something bigger—your project, a previous concept, real-world examples, or your own experience.

**Example Prompt:** "How does this week's reading connect to what I'm working on?"

---

### 🔧 The Troubleshooter
**Definition:** Document a challenge you faced and how you worked through it—technical, conceptual, or practical.

**Example Prompt:** "What was difficult this week, and how did I solve it?"

---

### 🎯 The Critic
**Definition:** Ask a critical question about the material. Challenge an assumption, identify a gap, or explore an implication.

**Example Prompt:** "What question or assumption from this week intrigues or concerns me?"

---

## Requirements

- **Length:** 250-300 words
- **Due:** Every Friday by 11:59 PM
- **Choose ONE approach** and delete the other two
- **Tag:** Include `#journal #weekX` at the bottom

[This chapter made me realize one of the biggest challenges isn't just doing the assignment but it's figuring out exactly what I'm trying to ask to begin with. I've fallen into the trap of asking questions that are way too broad. This chapter made me see that even if I gather good data and put in effort, a weak question can make the entire project fall apart. Something I took from this chapter is that a strong research question needs to be specific and measurable. I should start narrowng things down. Instead of asking something broad about music and emotions, I should focus on a relationship between variables, like how a certain type of music affects a specific group of people. This makes the project feel more manageable and actually possible to complete. The idea of operationalization stuck out to me too. I now understand that I have to define exactly how I'm measuring concepts, whether it's through surveys, behavior, or some type of data analysis. I can no longer rely on general ideas anymore. Overall, this reading helped shift my mindset from just picking a topic to carefully designing a question that can actually be answered. The quality of my question will determine how successful the rest of my project is, so spending more time refining it is actually worth it.]
