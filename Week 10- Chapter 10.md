# Weekly Journal Entry Guide

## How to Approach Your Reflection

Each week, you'll reflect on course material using **one of three approaches**. Pick the one that connects most to what you're thinking about this week.

---

## The Three Approaches

### 🔗 The Connector
**Definition:** Link this week's material to something bigger—your project, a previous concept, real-world examples, or your own experience.

**Example Prompt:** "How does this week's reading connect to what I'm working on?"


---

## Requirements

- **Length:** 250-300 words
- **Due:** Every Friday by 11:59 PM
- **Choose ONE approach** and delete the other two
- **Tag:** Include `#journal #weekX` at the bottom

## Your Reflection

[A codebook is so important for making research clear and consistent. I didn't realize how much detail goes into creating rules for coding data but it makes sense why that much attention to detail is needed. Coding to me was based on personal judgement but now I see that without clear instructions, different people could get very different results. This connects to my project because I now understand that the variables might not be enough on their own. I need a codebook that explains exactly how to apply them. For example, I can't just say positive or negative when coding song sentiment. I need to explain what counts as each category and what to do in situations where it could be considered sarcasm or mixed emotions. Without these rules, my results wouldn't be reliable. This chapter also connected to the idea of operationalization from Chapter 9. I learned about defining variables and now I'm learning how to turn those definitions into step by step instructions. Comparing a codebook to a computer program helped me understand this better. The program needs clear commands and coders near clear rules. Good research depends not only on ideas but also on clear rules. Me having a strong codebook helps make the research more reliable and easier for others to understand and repeat.  ]
